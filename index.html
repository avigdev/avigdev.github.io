I am the lead researcher in TaKaDu, a central event management system for water distribution networks. Our machine learning engine includes a complex transformers engine, and a set of classifiers and physical models that give water utilities an up-to-date online status of their network in terms of leakages, data problems, pump failures, valve breakages etc.



I'm currently working on:

  • Asset management model for pipes
  • Prediction model for pump failures
  • Bayesian model for water supply timeseries
  • Water supply data cleaner
  • Hybrid statistical and physical model of a water distribution network

My interests include : Timeseries analysis, anomaly detection, recommender systems and complex networks

I was also one of the initiators of the field of climate networks

I previously worked on : El-Nino and La-Nina statistical predictions, teleconnections, double diffusion, ocean dynamics and instabilities, stochastic transport
